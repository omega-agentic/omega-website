## Core Principles

### Lead with what we build, never with what others don't.

We have the best technology. That's enough. We never name competitors. We never compare unfavorably. We never use language that positions us *against* anyone. We position ourselves *ahead*.

**Yes:**
> Built in Haskell. Renders in microseconds.

**No:**
> ~~Not another React-in-terminal abomination.~~

**Yes:**
> Derived from first principles. Formally verified.

**No:**
> ~~Unlike [competitor] which is just a wrapper around broken abstractions.~~

If someone asks how we differ from X, we describe what we do. The contrast is implicit. The reader is smart enough to draw their own conclusions.

### Respect everyone in the ecosystem.

The people who built the current stack â€” the ML researchers, the API designers, the framework authors â€” were solving hard problems under real constraints. They optimized for what mattered at the time. We have the advantage of building later, with different constraints. That's not superiority. That's timing.

When we describe the state of the current infrastructure, we describe *what is*, not *who's to blame*. The wire protocol is broken. That's a fact about the wire protocol, not an indictment of the people who shipped it.

**Yes:**
> The wire protocol emerged from the intersection of SSE, early API design, and rapid iteration. It served its purpose. Now agents need something more.

**No:**
> ~~The wire protocol is garbage shipped by people who didn't know what they were doing.~~

### Prove, don't claim.

Every assertion should be backed by something: a number, a benchmark, a theorem, a line of code. If we can't back it, we don't say it.

**Yes:**
> 32 theorems proven in Lean4. 0 sorry. Compiles against mathlib 4.26.

**No:**
> ~~We have the most advanced formally verified infrastructure in the industry.~~

Show the proof. Let the reader decide what it means.

## Formatting Rules

### Capitalization

- **Tagline:** Sentence case with period. "The end state of intelligence."
- **Sign-off:** All lowercase, no period. "the result is saved"
- **Product names:** Omega Boost, Omega Code, Omega SDK (capitalized)
- **Section labels:** ALL CAPS, JetBrains Mono, wide tracking
- **Headlines:** Sentence case. No title case. "Your AI coding tools are broken." not "Your AI Coding Tools Are Broken."

### Punctuation

- Periods, not exclamation marks. Ever. The conviction is in the content.
- Em dashes for asides â€” used sparingly.
- No ellipses in marketing copy. Say the thing or don't.

### Numbers

- Always use numerals: 32, not thirty-two. 650, not six hundred fifty.
- Use the â†’ arrow for before/after: 650 â†’ 1.5
- Units attached: <1ms, 50MB, 1397 TFLOPS

## The Test

Before publishing any copy, ask:

1. **Is every claim backed by a number, a proof, or a line of code?** If not, cut it or back it.
2. **Are we describing what we build, or what others don't?** If the latter, rewrite.
3. **Would we be embarrassed by this sentence in five years?** If yes, cut it.
4. **Can this be shorter?** It can. Make it shorter.
5. **Does this waste the reader's time?** They have <1ms tolerance. Same as our proxy.
